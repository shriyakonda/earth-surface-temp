What you're looking at

You're looking at readings from Terra, a NASA Satellite that has been measuring the temperature of the Earth every month. Terra specifically measures the temperature of the ground itself; sand, rock, forest, canopy, ice, etc, which is different from the air temperature. This distinction is important because land surface temperature is what the planet is actually radiating. We can use it to predict weather, storms and evaporation, and see things that ground stations miss entirely, such as temperatures of remote stretches of deserts or even scorching parking lots where no one is keeping track of temperature.
